The World's History of Flight Incidents

Ever since the Wright brothers decided to take the world into the skies in the early 1900s, aviation has been a major industry for transportation and entertainment alike. On any given day around eight million people fly on an airplane and each year companies work to develop new components designed to improve the experience of flying. Unfortunately, as with every advancement of technology, the miraculous benefits of such creation are sprinkled with instances of devastating failure.

Using data provided to us concerning flight incidents over twenty-one years, we plan to take you through time and explore possible reasoning for patterns that emerge. Clicking the “Next” button will transition you to the next visualization. You can always select the “Previous” button to return to a past visual. Dragging your mouse over a point will display more details about that specific data.
